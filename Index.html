Lanciano, Italy

Circa 700-750 AD

The Account:

According to tradition, a Basilian monk celebrating Mass in the Church of St. Legontian experienced doubts about the real presence of Christ in the Eucharist. During the consecration, the host reportedly transformed into visible flesh, and the wine became visible blood.

8th Century
Original miracle occurs during Mass
1574
Relics transferred to Church of St. Francis
1970-1981
Scientific investigations conducted

Physical Characteristics:

  • The flesh: brown in color, roughly circular, about the size of a large host
  • The blood: crystallized into five pellets of different sizes
  • Both relics have been preserved for over 1,200 years without decay
  • No trace of preservatives or mummification agents found

üî¨ Scientific Investigations

Lanciano Studies (1970-1981):

Led by: Professor Odoardo Linoli (University of Siena)

Key Findings:

  • Flesh Analysis: Identified as cardiac muscle tissue from the left ventricle of a human heart
  • Blood Analysis: Human blood, Type AB
  • Protein Structure: Same proteins found in fresh blood
  • Preservation: No evidence of artificial preservation or mummification
  • Age Consistency: Both samples show identical blood type and protein markers

Santar√©m Studies:

While less extensively studied than Lanciano, preliminary analyses have been conducted on the blood-stained veil and surrounding materials.

Observations:

  • Blood stains remain vivid after 750+ years
  • No signs of artificial coloring or painting detected
  • Pattern consistent with liquid blood absorption
  • Preservation without environmental controls deemed unusual

ü§î Scientific Perspective:

While these findings are documented, the scientific community maintains that peer review and independent verification are essential for any extraordinary claims. The Catholic Church presents these as matters of faith, not scientific proof of supernatural events.
